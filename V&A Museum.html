Victoria & Albert Museum

HISTORY


The V&A is a collection of museums devoted to the power of creativity. They portray the 5,000-year-old narrative of creation via exhibits, events, educational programs, digital experiences, conservation, research and an ever-changing national collection of over 2.8 million artefacts from all creative genres.

The history of the V&A is unlike any other. From its founding as a Museum of Manufactures in 1852 to the foundation stone imposed by Queen Victoria in 1899. Also, to today’s revolutionary galleries, the museum’s collection and public interpretation of art and design have changed continuously. Its treasures cover almost 5,000 years of human invention in almost every media and are displayed in one of Britain’s greatest collections of Victorian and contemporary architecture.

THINGS TO KNOW BEFORE YOU GO

The information as below:
Operation
  • Open daily at 10:00 a.m. to 17:45 p.m.
  • Friday at 10:00 a.m.-22:00 p.m. (some galleries close after 17:45 p.m.)
Location
 Cromwell Road, London, SW7 2RL
Tickets
 There is no charge for admission and reservations are not required but some exhibits need tickets.
How to get there
  • Earls Court is the nearest step-free underground station which is 1.2 miles away.
  • London Victoria station is roughly a step-free which is 1.3 miles away.
Access
  • Borrowing mobility aids
    • - The information desk in the Grand Entrance provides wheelchairs and walking sticks.Book at least 24 hours in advance.
  • Portable stools
    • - Pick up stools from a variety of galleries.
  • Ear defenders for children
    • - Two sets of child-sized ear defenders are available to be used at the Learning Centre desk. There is no need to book.
